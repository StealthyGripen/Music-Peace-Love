From a broom,

Playing in many small acts,
he inevitably got kicked out
owing to his unique play style,
which wasn’t yet agreeable for everyone.

around

“So like, I was

Jumping between several bands,
playing as a backing musician,
he picked up a great deal of experience,
but eventually felt he outgrew that scene.

Meeting the right people,

on the music scene across the Atlantic,

Following the formation of
The Jimi Hendrix Experience,

the master guitar player

Monterey Pop Music Festival

Summoning flames from his guitar,
he outdid The Who’s
stage wrecking antics at the
Monterey Pop Music Festival,
aided by a tin of lighter fluid.

“Yeah but
all that
has to come from
inside
you know.”

“You have to use
fantasy
in order to
show different
sides of
reality.”

Taking listeners to different realities, he began to show interest in science
fiction and outer space, following
ground-breaking the release of
Axis: Bold as Love.

One of his smashed guitars at the EMP Museum

Known to have a violent streak,
aggravated by alcohol consumption,
he often smashed things,
notably some of his guitars, onstage.

“I didn’t know
it was
anger
until they
told me
it was.”

“I hate to
be in a corner,
I’d hate to be
put as only
a guitar player
or either only as a
songwriter,
or only as a
tapdancer,
or something
like that.”

The release of
Electric Ladyland,
highlighted his arduous perfectionism,
which demanded repeated takes,
and led to deterioration in kinship between band members.

69

The Experience shortly before breaking up

Owing to his taxing work ethic,
and domineering control over the band,
The Experience split up,
playing their final performance at the
Denver Pop Festival.

“You’re
gonna have
the
other side
of
everything.”

It’s really
up to the people,
if
they want to keep it going,
they’ll
keep it going.”

In a watershed moment of the
counter-culture, his rendition of
“The Star Spangled Banner”,
performed at the landmark
Woodstock Festival,
immortalised him as a legend in the annals of rock history.
